Birulka

An ancient Belarusian folk game with a ball and a tilted board

One of the most popular folk games of the Hrodna and Brest regions. Known since the 17th century, birulka remains popular at village fairs to this day.

History

From a clay ball to a modern game

Birulka is a traditional Belarusian tabletop game in which the player launches a ball down a tilted wooden board with holes. Each hole represents an animal of the Belovezhskaya Pushcha and awards a different number of points.

The earliest mentions of birulka are found in records of the Hrodna county from the late 17th century. Ethnographer Mikhail Fedorowski, in his collection "The Belarusian People" (1897), describes the game as "an amusement with a ball on a board with pits, which children make from alder bark and clay."

"Girls and boys would gather in the house, set the board on a log, and whoever first got the ball into the bison hole — grandmother would give them the first pancake." — Record from the village of Gudevichi, Mosty district, 1923 (archive of IMEF NAS of Belarus)

The birulka board was carved from alder — a tree that grows abundantly in Belarusian marshlands. The balls were shaped from clay and fired in a stove. In wealthier families, balls were made of glass or even amber.

Game rules

How to play birulka

Setup

1
Set the board at a tilt (5–7 degrees). Traditionally, a log was placed under the back edge.
2
Place a clay (or glass) ball in the launch chute at the bottom right.
3
Pull the plunger down and release — the ball shoots onto the playing field.
4
The ball bounces off the pin bumpers and lands in one of the holes.
5
The number of points depends on which hole the ball lands in.

Scoring zones

Traditionally, the holes were named after animals of the Belovezhskaya Pushcha. The rarer the animal — the more points:

100
🦬 Bison
75
🫎 Moose
50
🦌 Deer
25
🐇 Hare

The first player to reach 500 points is declared the "Forest King" (or "Forest Queen"). At traditional fairs, the winner received a clay whistle shaped like a bison.

Workshop

Build a birulka at home

A modern version of birulka can be made from simple materials — cardboard, skewers, and a spring. It makes a great school or family project!

Materials

📦
Corrugated cardboard (2 sheets, A3) — for the base and sides
🔩
Compression spring (Ø8–10 mm, length 5 cm)
🔮
Glass marble Ø16 mm
🪡
Wooden skewers (10–12 pcs.) for pin bumpers
